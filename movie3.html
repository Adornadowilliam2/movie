Synopsis

The movie "To Tell the Truth" begins with an episode of the popular game show 'To Tell the Truth' set in 1977. Three contestants appear claiming to be the legendary Frank William Abagnale, who impersonated an airline pilot, a lawyer, and a doctor, and scammed people on three continents for millions of dollars before reaching the age of nineteen. Frank reveals the name of Carl Hanratty, an FBI agent. The film then shifts to 1969, with FBI agent Carl Hanratty arriving at a French prison to extradite a flu-stricken Frank Abagnale Jr. Frank attempts to escape but is too sick to even make it outside. The film then flashes back to six years earlier, where 16-year-old Frank Abagnale Jr. lives in New Rochelle, New York with his father Frank Abagnale, Sr. (Christopher Walken), and French mother Paula (Nathalie Baye). Frank's father takes him to a store where Frank watches as his father cons a woman into lending him a black suit for Frank Jr. Frank drives his father to the Chase Manhattan Bank dressed with the suit and pretending to be his chauffeur, in a ruse to impress the bank people and secure a loan. When the loan is denied, the family is forced to move from their grand home to a small apartment, causing tension between Frank's parents. Frank tries to con his way into transportation and shelter, using his checkbook to con his way into transportation and shelter. He eventually runs away from home, using his checkbook to con his way into transportation and shelter. Carl Hanratty, a persistent FBI agent, tracks down Frank's check fraud and arrests him. Frank pretends to be Agent "Barry Allen" of the United States Secret Service, but fingerprints collected in the room lead nowhere. Frank attempts to use the money he has stolen to find a way to reunite his divorced parents, but Carl realizes that Frank has no one else to talk to. Carl's men search for runaways in the New York area, eventually leading them to Frank's mother, who has now remarried to Paula.
